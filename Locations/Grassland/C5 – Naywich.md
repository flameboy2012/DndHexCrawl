**Tags:** Road access, inn, market, temple, quest hub

_Town / Settlement Hub_ A modest market town of ~400 souls, built around a central well and a small temple to a harvest deity. Known for its weekly grain market and decent inn, _The Furrow & Flagon_. The town watch is thinly stretched. Lady Hallowstride's influence is felt here—not always welcomed.

### The Golden Bough Inn & Tavern

The heart of town social life, known for its house cider pressed from the orchards just east of town. The common room smells of woodsmoke and fermenting apples. The owner, **Marta Hollowell**, is a broad-shouldered widow in her fifties who runs the place with calm authority. She knows every rumor in town and will share them freely with paying guests. Her teenage son **Pip** works the tables and is desperately eager to hear stories from travelers — he's been asking questions about the road east for months now.

---

### The Naywich Granary & Trade Post

The largest building in town, doubling as a general store and the closest thing to a bank the settlement has. Run by **Aldous Fenn**, a meticulous and somewhat nervous man who keeps obsessive ledgers. He's recently noticed discrepancies in the grain tallies — some stores going missing in the night — and is too afraid to report it to anyone official. He'll quietly pay the party to look into it.

---

### The Shrine of the Tillbound

A small stone chapel dedicated to a minor agricultural deity of the kingdom's rural folk. It's tended by **Sister Orvaine**, an elderly woman who came out to the borderlands as a young missionary decades ago and simply never left. She's sharp, kind, and deeply connected to the local farming families. She also quietly performs blessings on travelers heading into the wilds, and she keeps a private journal of strange omens she's been recording over the past two seasons.

---

### The Farrier & Smithy

A working smithy that does mostly practical work — horseshoes, plow fittings, tool repairs. **Bram Cutler** is the smith, a taciturn half-orc man with a reputation for honesty and fair prices. He's one of the most respected people in town despite being an outsider. He's also a veteran of a border skirmish from years past and can be a useful source of information about the terrain and what used to live in it.

---

### The Warden's Post

A sturdy two-room building that serves as the seat of local law. **Warden Seska Thorne** is a former soldier appointed by the kingdom to keep order on the frontier. She's competent and dedicated but deeply understaffed — she has two deputies and that's it. She's pragmatic about adventurers and will offer work, but she has little patience for trouble. She's been receiving reports from outlying farms of livestock going missing and wants answers more than she wants to admit.

---

### Greaves Farm (on the edge of town)

The oldest farmstead in Naywich, run by the elderly **Corvin and Dulla Greaves** and their adult children. Their farm borders the wild hexes and their family has been here longer than the town itself. They're superstitious, knowledgeable about local lore, and quietly convinced that something has changed in the woods to the north. Corvin will trade local knowledge for help with work or protection.

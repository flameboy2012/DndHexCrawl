_Major Location / Dragon Lair_

A vast rocky summit perpetually wreathed in low cloud and river mist. What was once thought to be a crumbling watchtower is in fact the entrance to the lair of Jiaolong, an ancient river dragon of eastern legend. Serpentine and vast, Jiaolong is covered in iridescent blue-green scales that shimmer like water in sunlight. Elaborate bone and driftwood arrangements surround the lair entrance, and the sound of rushing water echoes from deep within.

The river that flows south through the grasslands — passing the Shattered Keep and Mother Dendra's Hovel — originates here, bubbling up from a sacred spring deep within Jiaolong's lair. Whether the dragon created the spring or merely claimed it is unknown. Jiaolong is ancient, proud, and not immediately hostile, but deeply territorial. He speaks in a slow, formal manner and considers the river and everything along its banks to be his domain. He is aware of Mother Dendra and tolerates her presence.

Approaching the lair without an offering of tribute is considered deeply unwise by those few who know of his existence. Thornshadow in A2 knows Jiaolong is there and has kept the secret deliberately, fearing what would happen if Naywich sent adventurers to slay him.

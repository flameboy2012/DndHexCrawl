_Dungeon / Minor Location_

A mossy stone trapdoor half buried in the forest floor, its iron ring green with age. The stonework around it is faintly warm to the touch regardless of the weather — a detail that unsettles those who notice it. Below lies a small vault of Yuan-ti construction, three chambers of dressed sandstone that feel entirely at odds with the cold northern forest surrounding them. How it came to be here, so far from the desert, is one of its many unanswered questions.

The first chamber is collapsed and scattered with bones and the remnants of whatever was once stored here — clay vessels, rotted cloth, and fragments of stone tablets carved with a serpent devouring its own tail. Those who recognise the sigil will know it as Beha's personal mark, though such knowledge is rare in the north.

The second chamber contains a stone lectern bearing a partially intact journal written in Yuan-ti script. The legible sections describe a northern expedition undertaken two thousand years ago at Beha's direct command — its purpose redacted by a careful hand. The final entries grow fearful in tone.

The third chamber is sealed by a stone door carved with a coiling serpent in relief. Behind it something still stirs. Whatever Beha left to guard this place has had two thousand years of patience. The chamber itself contains a single stone chest bearing the same serpent-devouring-its-tail sigil found throughout the pyramid far to the south — its contents unknown.
